## Output

To calculate compound interest, we can use the formula `A = P * (1 + r/n)^(nt)`, where `A` is the amount of money accumulated after `n` years, including interest, `P` is the principal amount (initial investment), `r` is the annual interest rate (in decimal), `n` is the number of times that interest is compounded per year, and `t` is the time the money is invested for in years.

```python
def calculate_compound_interest(principal, annual_interest_rate, years, compounding_frequency=1):
    """
    Calculate compound interest.

    Args:
    - principal (float): The initial investment.
    - annual_interest_rate (float): The annual interest rate (in decimal).
    - years (int): The time the money is invested for in years.
    - compounding_frequency (int, optional): The number of times that interest is compounded per year. Defaults to 1.

    Returns:
    - float: The amount of money accumulated after the given years, including interest.
    """
    amount = principal * (1 + annual_interest_rate/compounding_frequency)**(compounding_frequency*years)
    return amount

# Example usage:
if __name__ == "__main__":
    principal = 1000  # Initial investment of $1000
    annual_interest_rate = 0.05  # 5% annual interest rate
    years = 5  # Investment period of 5 years
    compounding_frequency = 12  # Compounded monthly

    result = calculate_compound_interest(principal, annual_interest_rate, years, compounding_frequency)
    print(f"The amount after {years} years is: ${result:.2f}")
```
