## THE REFRAME

TTLG is not a diagnostic tool. It is a matchmaker.

The diagnostic flow is the intake interview. The 7-track analysis is the compatibility assessment. The Build Manifests are the match results. The Three-Option Engagement Menu is the proposal. And the certified node catalog — 19 marketplace-ready sovereign nodes — is the inventory of solutions available to match against any gap discovered.

The client never browses a product catalog. They describe their pain, and TTLG assembles the solution.

## HOW THE MATCHMAKING WORKS

### Step 1: The Client Describes Their World

Through any entry point — a discovery call (captured by FOTW), a content engagement (tracked by Echolocation), a direct diagnostic request, or a conversational TTLG session — the client describes their business, their pain, and their goals.

CCP disambiguates their language through concentric ring analysis. "I can't keep up" becomes a specific dimensional mapping: Service Track → scheduling capacity, with a secondary signal in Financial Health → headcount constraint. The system understands what they mean, not just what they say.

### Step 2: TTLG Maps the Gaps

The 7-track diagnostic (expandable to 25 layers for enterprise) scans across:

**Consumer-facing (Layers 1-10):** Marketing, Sales, Service, Governance, Financial Health, Reputation, Advocacy

**Organizational-facing (Layers 11-15):** Internal ops, team communication, knowledge management, process documentation, change readiness

**Partner-facing (Layers 16-20):** Supply chain, vendor dependency, partnership pipeline, co-marketing, ecosystem interoperability

**Strategic (Layers 21-25):** Board reporting, IP management, regulatory compliance, M&A readiness, succession planning

Each gap produces:
- A consequence chain (what this gap costs, in dollars and downstream effects)
- A severity classification
- A cost-of-inaction estimate (Value at Risk)

### Step 3: The Surgeon Matches Gaps to Nodes

This is the matchmaking step. For every gap identified, the Surgeon consults:

1. **The Node Catalog** — 19 marketplace-ready sovereign nodes, each with defined capabilities, pricing, and configuration requirements
2. **The Cost Heuristics** — sector-specific cost models that estimate the dollar impact of each gap
3. **The Build Manifest Library** — pre-compiled deployment specifications for each node

The Surgeon produces three prescriptions per track:
- **Quick Win** — smallest intervention, fastest ROI, lowest cost
- **Strategic Build** — balanced investment, 90-day transformation
- **Full Transformation** — comprehensive deployment, maximum impact

Each prescription includes the specific EPOS node(s) that solve the gap, the estimated configuration time, the projected ROI, and a machine-readable Build Manifest that says exactly what gets deployed.

### Step 4: The Mirror Report Shows Both Sides

The Mirror Report is the looking glass. One side shows the client their current reality — the gaps, the costs, the risks. The other side shows what their business looks like with the matched EPOS nodes deployed — the automation, the intelligence, the savings.

Two versions serve two audiences:
- **Boardroom View:** Revenue leakage, ROI projections, timeline — for the decision-maker
- **Engine Room View:** Technical debt, Build Manifests, deployment architecture — for the implementer

### Step 5: The Three-Option Engagement Menu

Every report ends with three doors:
- **Quick Win:** $X, Y hours, addresses the highest-leverage gap
- **Strategic Build:** $X, Y days, addresses the top 3-5 gaps with a 90-day plan
- **Full Transformation:** $X, Y weeks, comprehensive EPOS deployment across all identified gaps

The client chooses a door. The engagement manifest activates the matched nodes. The business process begins.

### Step 6: The Loop Closes

FOTW captures the delivery process. The Weekly Market View maintains the relationship. Health scoring monitors satisfaction. The cross-sell engine identifies the next gaps. And when the client's score improves from the first engagement, the score delta becomes the case study that attracts the next client.

Every engagement teaches the system. The cost heuristics get more accurate. The matchmaking gets more precise. The prescriptions get more surgical. The pattern library grows. The next match is better than the last.

## WHY MATCHMAKING BEATS CATALOGS

**Product catalog approach:** "Here are 19 products. Read the descriptions. Pick one. Hope it fits."
- Client burden: high (they have to understand what each product does)
- Conversion rate: low (decision paralysis with 19 options)
- Fit accuracy: low (client guesses what they need)

**Matchmaker approach:** "Tell us about your business. We'll show you exactly which combination fills your gaps, at what cost, with what ROI."
- Client burden: zero (they describe pain, TTLG does the rest)
- Conversion rate: high (proposal is tailored to their specific situation)
- Fit accuracy: high (diagnostic evidence drives the match, not guesswork)

The matchmaker approach also produces a natural upsell path. The Quick Win addresses the urgent gap. Once it's working, the score delta proves value. The client sees the remaining gaps still costing them money. The Strategic Build becomes obvious. And by the time the Full Transformation is on the table, the client has experienced enough value that the investment is justified by evidence, not sales pressure.
