# Signal Framework — Dream 100 + FOTW

## Why generic content marketing doesn't work anymore

Five years ago you could win by posting consistently. Today everyone
posts consistently. The winner is the person whose content feels like
it was written about the reader's exact current problem — because it
was. That requires listening more than publishing.

The Signal Framework is how you listen. It has two halves:

1. **Dream 100** — a curated list of 100 voices who define your market.
2. **FOTW** — a discipline for extracting signal from what they say
   before it becomes public narrative.

---

## Part 1 — The Dream 100

The Dream 100 is a classic direct-response concept: the 100 people,
organizations, or channels whose audience IS your audience. Chet
Holmes's original version was about who to sell TO. FOTW flips it:
the Dream 100 is who to LISTEN to.

Why 100: fewer than 30 is too narrow (you miss divergent voices); more
than 200 is too many to maintain (signal drowns). ~100 is the band
where you can actually notice patterns.

### How to build yours

Five categories × 20 each = 100:

1. **Creators in your niche** — 20 people regularly publishing in your
   space. Half big, half small.
2. **Operators** — 20 people doing the work you do, publishing their
   practice (e.g., ops leads, founders, CRO leads)
3. **Adjacent experts** — 20 people from adjacent spaces whose frame
   informs yours (psychology, economics, design, etc.)
4. **Communities** — 20 subreddits, Discords, Slack groups, Skool
   communities where your audience talks to itself
5. **Sources** — 20 publications, podcasts, newsletters that shape the
   narrative

Put them in a spreadsheet. Subscribe, follow, or watch each.

### What to record

For each of the 100, a one-line record of **what they publish**, and
**what their audience reacts to.** Not just what's posted — what's
engaged with. Engagement is the primary signal.

## Part 2 — FOTW (First Of Their Watch)

A "watch" is a 24-hour window during which a specific community is
discussing something. FOTW means **you noticed the watch before the
community consensus formed** — you're the first to their watch.

FOTW isn't about being first to publish. It's about being first to
**notice the shift** so that when you publish two days later, your
content lands into a market that's pre-warmed.

### The FOTW extraction process

Every 24-48 hours, scan your Dream 100. Look for:

1. **Pain language** — new phrasings of old problems. When 3+ sources
   start using the same new phrase for a familiar pain, it's surfacing.
2. **Feature requests** — things the audience says "I wish X existed"
   about. These are future products.
3. **Pricing sensitivity** — language like "I'd pay $X" or "it's not
   worth $Y" across multiple voices. Anchor prices that are forming.
4. **Competitor weaknesses** — complaints with specific names attached.
   Competitors' open flanks.
5. **Unmet needs** — questions repeatedly asked in communities that
   never get satisfactory answers. These are content opportunities.

### The signal log

Per signal you find, record:
- **Signal type** (pain, feature, price, competitor, unmet)
- **Verbatim** (exact phrasing, source link)
- **Dream 100 number** (where you saw it)
- **Frequency** (1st time? 3rd time in a week?)
- **Confidence** (1–10: gut call on whether this is a real shift)
- **Proposed response** (one-line content idea)

Over time the log becomes a research database for your content engine.

### The rhythm

- Daily: 15-min scan (1-2 signals per day average)
- Weekly: 60-min synthesis (which 3 signals do you act on?)
- Monthly: pattern audit (what's trending up? what died?)

## Part 3 — From signal to content

The discipline: **do not publish content that doesn't map to a logged
signal.** Content made-up-from-nowhere goes into the void. Content
tied to a real signal lands.

Specifically:

- Every piece of content references ≥ 1 signal log entry.
- Every signal log entry that reaches "frequency ≥ 3, confidence ≥ 7"
  is a content mandate — you MUST produce a piece within 7 days.
- Every piece that goes live is measured at T+24h and T+48h
  (echolocation — see the CCP Pack for the scoring framework).

---

## Part 4 — What FOTW is NOT

- It's not SEO. You're not optimizing for search. You're optimizing
  for being in the room when the conversation shifts.
- It's not trend-chasing. A trend has already peaked. FOTW is pre-trend.
- It's not a substitute for your own voice. The framework tells you
  *what* the market is noticing. Your voice is how you respond to it.
