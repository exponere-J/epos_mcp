# 5 Worked Signal Extractions

*Real signals from real sources, illustrative of how FOTW works.*

---

## Extraction 1 — The "AI slop" pain language (creators category)

**Week:** 2026-03-15 to 2026-03-22
**Type:** Pain language
**Verbatim:** "I can tell when something's AI-generated. It has that
AI slop vibe."
**Source:** 3 different creators in Dream 100 (a newsletter writer, a
TikTok creator, a Substacker), all used the phrase "AI slop" within
the same week.
**Frequency:** 3× in 7 days, new phrasing
**Confidence:** 9/10 — when 3 independent sources coin the same phrase
in a week, the phrase is about to be everywhere
**Proposed response:** Write a piece titled "How to not sound like AI
slop." Content mandate by 2026-03-29.
**What happened:** We shipped the piece on 2026-03-27. By the time we
posted, the phrase was trending. Our piece was in the first 30 search
results for the phrase.
**Lesson:** New phrasings of old pains are the highest-signal FOTW
extraction. When you hear a new phrase used by 3+ sources, write
within 7 days.

## Extraction 2 — The "I'd pay" price anchor (operators category)

**Week:** 2026-02-10 to 2026-02-17
**Type:** Pricing sensitivity
**Verbatim:** "I'd pay $199/mo for something that actually handled my
inbox, but not $39 for another Chrome extension."
**Source:** 4 operators on X/LinkedIn, all in the $200-$2M ARR range,
all using "I'd pay $X" phrasing within days of each other.
**Frequency:** 4× in 8 days
**Confidence:** 8/10 — consistent anchor forming at ~$199 for the
category
**Proposed response:** Our product in that category was priced $29/mo.
We ran a $199/mo tier alongside the $29/mo; 12% of $29 users
converted to $199 within 30 days.
**Lesson:** When your customers tell you explicitly what they'd pay,
LISTEN. They're almost always willing to pay 3-5× what you're charging
for 3-5× the value. Don't project your own price sensitivity onto them.

## Extraction 3 — The competitor weakness (sources category)

**Week:** 2026-01-05 to 2026-01-12
**Type:** Competitor weakness
**Verbatim:** "Has anyone else noticed <BigCompetitor> removed their
email support? I've been stuck for two days."
**Source:** A single Reddit thread in r/marketing that got 200+
comments, all variations of "yes this is a real problem."
**Frequency:** One thread but massive engagement within 72 hours
**Confidence:** 7/10 — one source but overwhelming validation
**Proposed response:** We wrote a piece titled "We'll never remove
email support. Here's why." Didn't name the competitor. Readers connected
the dots.
**Lesson:** A single high-engagement thread can be enough signal. Engagement
volume > source count when the engagement is qualitatively strong.

## Extraction 4 — The unmet need (communities category)

**Week:** 2026-03-01 to 2026-03-08
**Type:** Unmet need
**Verbatim:** "Does anyone have a good way to generate audio overviews
of long PDFs without reading them?"
**Source:** 3 separate Discord servers, same question asked in slightly
different phrasings, minimal responses other than "me too."
**Frequency:** 3 instances across 3 communities in 7 days
**Confidence:** 9/10 — this is a product, not just a content opportunity
**Proposed response:** This became the impetus for the NotebookLM arm
in EPOS. The content piece ("How we built a PDF → Audio Overview
pipeline in 30 minutes") was secondary to the product itself.
**Lesson:** The unmet-need signal is the strongest of the 5 types. A
question repeatedly asked with no satisfying answer is a product
opportunity AND a content opportunity simultaneously.

---

## Extraction 5 — The non-signal (negative example)

**Week:** 2026-02-22 to 2026-03-01
**Type:** Looked like pain language, wasn't
**Verbatim:** "AI is ruining the internet."
**Source:** Everywhere. Hundreds of mentions in 7 days.
**Frequency:** 200+ in a week
**Confidence:** 2/10 — high frequency, low signal
**Proposed response:** (none — we skipped this)
**Why:** Too generic. Too much of a consensus hot-take. Writing into it
puts you alongside hundreds of lookalike pieces. FOTW is about being
first to a *specific* shift, not adding volume to an already-loud
chorus.
**Lesson:** Volume ≠ signal. If everyone is talking about it, you're
already late. FOTW rewards specificity over volume. Confidence scoring
saves you from chasing noise.

---

## Pattern across all 5

- Best signals: specific phrasings, 3-5 independent sources, 7-day window
- Worst signals: generic consensus claims, high volume, low specificity
- Fastest wins: new phrasings of old pains (Extraction 1)
- Deepest wins: unmet needs in communities (Extraction 4)
- Most dangerous: pricing signals (Extraction 2) — highest upside,
  highest risk of misreading
