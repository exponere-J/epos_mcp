# Category 1 — Strategic Intent Extraction (4 templates)

*Use case: you have raw material (transcript, conversation log, meeting
notes) and you need a crisp strategic artifact. Flat prompts produce
generic summaries. CCP-ringed prompts produce artifacts that reflect the
specific strategic intent buried in the source.*

---

## Template 1.1 — The Transcript-to-Strategic-Brief

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is a one-page strategic brief for an executive who missed the
meeting. The executive has 90 seconds to read it. If something is not
load-bearing, it does not appear.

### `# SECTION — Decisions made`

Bullet list. Each bullet is a decision in the format:
`<decision>. <who owns it>. <by when>.`
Include only decisions that were actually made. Exclude discussion of
decisions that were deferred.

### `# SECTION — Open questions`

Bullet list of questions the meeting left unanswered. Format:
`<question>. <who needs to resolve>. <blocker if any>.`

### `# SECTION — Risks surfaced`

Bullet list. Each bullet: `<risk>. <probability: low/med/high>. <mitigation
discussed or NONE>.`

### `# SECTION — Next concrete action`

Exactly one sentence. The single next action that unblocks the most
downstream work. Name the owner.

### `# STATEMENT`

The brief must not editorialize. No "the team should consider" — only
"the team decided" or "the team did not decide."

### `# TOKEN`

Prefer: decided, deferred, blocker, owner, by <date>
Avoid: actually, literally, leverage, synergy, robust, actionable

## Template 1.2 — The Competitive Positioning Extract

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is a competitive positioning extract. It lives in my CRM. A rep
reads it in 30 seconds before a call. It tells them what the prospect
compared us to and what won or lost.

### `# SECTION — Comparison set`

Bullet list. Each bullet is one competitor the prospect mentioned.
Format: `<competitor>. <what the prospect valued about them>. <what the
prospect objected to>.`

### `# SECTION — Why we won / lost`

One paragraph. 3–5 sentences. Concrete, specific to this deal. Name
features if named, name people if named. Do not generalize.

### `# SECTION — Replayable quote`

One direct quote from the prospect that the rep can paraphrase on the
next similar call.

### `# TOKEN`

Prefer: specific person names, specific product names, dollar amounts,
dates. Avoid: "potential customer," "market leader," "enterprise-grade."

## Template 1.3 — The Founder Vision Crystallizer

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is a 200-word statement of founder vision. It will be read by a
prospective hire who is deciding whether to take a pay cut to join. The
statement answers "why are you building this, really?"

### `# SECTION — Origin moment`

One paragraph. First-person. A specific moment — a conversation, a
failure, an embodied experience — that seeded the work. No "I realized
that." Just the moment.

### `# SECTION — The conviction`

One paragraph. What the founder is convinced is true that most people
reject. Stated plainly.

### `# SECTION — The bet`

One sentence. "We're betting that <specific claim about the future>."

### `# TOKEN`

Prefer: first-person singular (I), present tense, concrete nouns.
Avoid: "we believe," "our mission," "journey," "transform," "empower."

## Template 1.4 — The Meeting-to-Decision-Journal Converter

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is a decision journal entry. It's for my future self to read in 6
months when I'm asking "why did I do that?"

### `# SECTION — The question`

One sentence stating the decision I faced.

### `# SECTION — What I chose`

One sentence stating what I picked.

### `# SECTION — Why (at the time)`

3–5 bullets. The reasons I gave myself. Honest ones.

### `# SECTION — What I'd need to see to know I was wrong`

2–3 bullets. Concrete, falsifiable signals.

### `# SECTION — Next check-in`

Date. "I'll re-read this on <date>."

### `# TOKEN`

Prefer: I (not we), chose (not selected), wrong (not suboptimal).
Avoid: "based on analysis," "after careful consideration," "strategic."

## How to pick the right template

| If you have... | Use... |
|---|---|
| A meeting transcript | 1.1 |
| A sales-call transcript or win/loss interview | 1.2 |
| Notes from a "why am I doing this?" session | 1.3 |
| Yourself thinking through a hard choice | 1.4 |
