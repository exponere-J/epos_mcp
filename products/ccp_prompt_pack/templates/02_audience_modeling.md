# Category 2 — Audience Modeling (4 templates)

*Use case: you have a product (or service, or offer) and you need to
write about it, position it, or sell it. The difference between
generic and specific is the audience model. Flat prompts default to
"small business owners"; CCP-ringed prompts produce an avatar that
sounds like one person you could call.*

---

## Template 2.1 — The Single-Person Avatar Builder

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is a profile of ONE specific person who would buy my product.
Not a segment. One human. The profile will be read by my content writer
to calibrate voice.

### `# SECTION — Identity`

Bullet list. `Name: <make one up>. Role: <specific, not "Marketing
Manager" — "Marketing Manager at a 20-person B2B SaaS that's just hit
$2M ARR">. Age range. City type.`

### `# SECTION — Day in the life`

One paragraph describing 8am to 9am on a weekday. What they check,
what they worry about, what relief they'd pay for.

### `# SECTION — Beliefs (operational)`

3 bullets. Things they're convinced are true that inform their
purchasing behavior. Not values. Working beliefs.

### `# SECTION — Objections (verbatim)`

3 bullets. The exact words they'd use to object. Not "concerns about
price" — "it looks overpriced for what it is."

### `# SECTION — What they'd text a friend about me`

One sentence. First-person-the-avatar. What they'd write if they found
my product and liked it.

### `# TOKEN`

Prefer: specific role titles, specific tools they use, specific
numbers. Avoid: "ideal customer," "target demographic," "persona."

## Template 2.2 — The Objection-Taxonomy Extractor

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is a taxonomy of the 5 most common objections to my product.
It's used by my rep team in the first 3 weeks of onboarding.

### `# SECTION — Per objection (repeat 5×)`

For each objection:

- **Objection name:** (short, memorable)
- **Verbatim:** (how a prospect actually says it)
- **Root cause:** (what the prospect is really worried about)
- **One-line response:** (what a rep says that defuses it)
- **Evidence to offer:** (proof artifact — case study, demo segment,
  testimonial)

### `# STATEMENT`

Order the 5 objections by frequency, not severity. Frequent-and-easy
beats rare-and-hard in training order.

### `# TOKEN`

Prefer: specific phrases from real calls. Avoid: "price objection,"
"feature request," "ROI concern."

## Template 2.3 — The Buyer-Journey Touchpoint Map

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is a map of the 10 touchpoints a buyer has with my brand, from
first heard-about-us to 90 days post-purchase. It informs my content
calendar.

### `# SECTION — Per touchpoint (repeat 10×)`

For each touchpoint:

- **Stage:** (Awareness / Interest / Desire / Action / Retention)
- **Touchpoint:** (the specific thing — "LinkedIn post they see on
  their feed")
- **Emotion arriving:** (what they feel right before this touchpoint)
- **Emotion leaving:** (what they feel right after — should move one
  notch toward purchase)
- **Content format:** (post, email, demo, audio overview)
- **Success signal:** (observable behavior that proves it worked)

### `# TOKEN`

Prefer: specific emotions (curious, skeptical, relieved, reassured),
specific channels. Avoid: "engaged," "activated," "converted."

## Template 2.4 — The Anti-Avatar (Who This Isn't For)

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is a description of the person who should NOT buy my product.
Explicitly. It will appear on my sales page under a "This isn't for
you if..." heading.

### `# SECTION — Three anti-profiles`

Each ~50 words. First-person addressed to the reader. Format:

"You want a cheaper alternative, not a better one. You'd pick the $9
version of anything. That's fine — but this isn't it."

### `# STATEMENT`

The anti-avatar must be someone who WOULD consider buying but
shouldn't. Not "people who don't need this at all." That's too easy.
The anti-avatar has to feel almost right.

### `# TOKEN`

Prefer: direct "you." Avoid: "we recommend," "not a fit," "our ideal."

## How to pick the right template

| If you need... | Use... |
|---|---|
| One calibrating avatar for content voice | 2.1 |
| A rep-training objection taxonomy | 2.2 |
| A content calendar keyed to buyer state | 2.3 |
| Anti-avatar copy for the sales page | 2.4 |
