# Category 3 — Content Production (4 templates)

*Use case: you have a thesis or a specific point to make, and you need
to produce a piece of content. These templates keep the thesis
load-bearing through every ring.*

---

## Template 3.1 — The One-Idea Essay

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is a 600-word essay. One idea. No extensions. No "and also." The
reader finishes thinking about the one idea, not the 3 sub-ideas I
crammed in.

### `# SECTION — Hook (≤60 words)`

A specific moment, object, or sentence that drops the reader into the
idea. No throat-clearing, no "in today's fast-paced world." Start in
the middle of something.

### `# SECTION — The idea (≤100 words)`

The thesis, stated plainly. One sentence of claim, two sentences of
the implication a reader might not have seen.

### `# SECTION — The evidence (≤300 words)`

2–3 concrete examples. Specific names, specific numbers, specific
moments. Not "studies show." Actually the thing.

### `# SECTION — The consequence (≤100 words)`

If the reader accepts the idea, what changes for them Monday morning?
Make it concrete and small, not aspirational and big.

### `# SECTION — The close (≤40 words)`

Do not summarize. Turn the reader toward their own experience. One
question, or one instruction.

### `# TOKEN`

Prefer: concrete nouns, specific dates, dollar amounts, present tense.
Avoid: "resonates," "landscape," "strategic imperative," "game-changer."

## Template 3.2 — The Three-Part Social Post

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is a LinkedIn or X post. 3 parts: hook, story, call. Total
≤1200 characters.

### `# SECTION — Hook (≤200 chars)`

One sentence or two. Must make the reader pause. Options: a counter-
intuitive claim, a concrete moment, a number that shouldn't be true.

### `# SECTION — Story (≤800 chars)`

3–5 short paragraphs. Problem → constraint → what the constraint
revealed. Specific details outsiders wouldn't guess.

### `# SECTION — Call (≤200 chars)`

Not "DM me." Invite someone who's felt the same thing. Or ask a
question whose answer I actually want.

### `# TOKEN`

Prefer: first-person singular, present tense, concrete verbs.
Avoid: "excited to share," "proud to announce," "hot take," "unpopular
opinion."

## Template 3.3 — The Case-Study Section (From a Larger Piece)

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is the "how we did it" section of a case study. It lives inside a
longer sales piece. It is not the whole piece — just this section.

### `# SECTION — Before state (≤150 words)`

Concrete. Name the specific pain with numbers. Example: "We were
sending 40 follow-up emails a week manually. Each took 8 minutes."

### `# SECTION — The intervention (≤300 words)`

What we did. Sequential. Each step starts with a verb. No adjectives
in step names.

### `# SECTION — After state (≤150 words)`

Measurable. Same metric frame as Before. "We're now sending the same
40 follow-ups in 12 minutes total."

### `# SECTION — Generalizable lesson (≤100 words)`

One sentence for the reader whose pain is similar but whose numbers
aren't the same.

### `# TOKEN`

Prefer: specific numbers, specific verbs (automated, rewired, moved,
deleted). Avoid: "streamlined," "transformed," "unlocked," "enabled."

## Template 3.4 — The Pitch-Email (First Touch)

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is a cold-outreach pitch email. 4 sentences. It goes to a person
I've researched. It's not a sequence — it's the first touch.

### `# SECTION — S1 (the trigger)`

One sentence. Specific thing the recipient did recently that I noticed.
Not "I saw your post" — which post, what about it.

### `# SECTION — S2 (the bridge)`

One sentence. What in my world connects to what they did.

### `# SECTION — S3 (the offer)`

One sentence. A specific, narrow thing I can give them in ≤15 minutes.

### `# SECTION — S4 (the ask)`

One sentence. A one-question ask. Not "would you like to chat?" —
"should I send the 3-paragraph brief?"

### `# TOKEN`

Prefer: the recipient's name used ≤1 time, first-person singular, short
words. Avoid: "I'd love to," "quick question," "circle back," "reach out."

---

## How to pick the right template

| If you need... | Use... |
|---|---|
| A one-idea essay / article | 3.1 |
| A social post that doesn't sound AI-generated | 3.2 |
| A case-study section for a longer piece | 3.3 |
| A cold-outreach pitch email | 3.4 |
