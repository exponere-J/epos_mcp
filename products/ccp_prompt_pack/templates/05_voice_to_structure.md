# Category 5 — Voice → Structure (4 templates)

*Use case: you dictated something on a walk, or you talked through an
idea on a call, or you have a long Slack thread with yourself. The raw
material is speech-shaped. These templates convert it into a
structured artifact without losing the voice.*

---

## Template 5.1 — The Walk-and-Talk Crystallizer

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is a 300-word crystallization of a 15-minute walk-and-talk. It
preserves the original voice (same word choices, same rhythm) but
imposes structure. The reader feels like they heard me talking, but
in an organized way.

### `# SECTION — Extract the claim`

From the transcript, identify the ONE claim the walk was about. State
it in the speaker's own words. Quote directly.

### `# SECTION — Preserve the reasoning order`

The speaker moved through reasoning steps in a specific order. Keep
that order. Don't reorder for logical elegance. The order reveals
thought.

### `# SECTION — Keep the specifics`

Every specific detail the speaker mentioned (names, places, numbers,
moments) stays. Even if it feels tangential, it was doing work in the
speaker's thinking.

### `# SECTION — Drop the throat-clearing`

Remove only: filler words ("um," "you know,"), repetitions of the
same point, and explicit meta-commentary ("anyway, so what I'm
saying"). Keep the content.

### `# TOKEN`

Use the speaker's vocabulary. If the speaker said "organism," don't
swap in "system." If they said "unblock," don't swap in "enable."

## Template 5.2 — The Slack-Thread-to-Decision-Doc

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is a decision document derived from a 40-message Slack thread.
The thread wandered. The doc doesn't. It tells a future reader what
was decided without making them reconstruct the conversation.

### `# SECTION — Question (inferred)`

One sentence. What the thread was really about. Not what the first
message said — what the thread actually wrestled with.

### `# SECTION — Positions represented`

Bullets. One per distinct position. Format: `<person>: <position>.
<their reason>.` Exclude positions stated once and withdrawn.

### `# SECTION — What was decided`

One sentence. Who decided. Include the timestamp if observable in the
thread.

### `# SECTION — Open (explicitly left open)`

Bullets. Things the thread acknowledged as not-yet-decided. With
owner.

### `# STATEMENT`

If the thread didn't actually reach a decision, say so. Don't
manufacture one.

### `# TOKEN`

Prefer: specific names from the thread, specific timestamps, direct
quotes for positions.

## Template 5.3 — The Idea-Burst-to-Outline

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is a structured outline derived from a stream-of-consciousness
idea-burst (voice memo, braindump doc, back-of-napkin). It preserves
the 2–3 best ideas and drops the rest. It becomes the skeleton for
tomorrow's writing.

### `# SECTION — Identify load-bearing ideas`

From the burst, find the 2 or 3 ideas that the others are hanging off.
These are load-bearing. Everything else is scaffolding.

### `# SECTION — Arrange into a minimal spine`

The load-bearing ideas become your outline headers. 2–3 headers, in the
order a reader would need them. Not the order you thought of them.

### `# SECTION — Attach anchors`

Under each header, list 1–2 specific anchors: a concrete example, a
quote, a number. These are what you'll write around tomorrow.

### `# SECTION — Name what you're dropping`

Bullets. Ideas in the burst that didn't make the cut. One-liners. This
list is your Nebula — they might come back later.

### `# TOKEN`

Keep the speaker's language. Do not rewrite ideas into "professional"
phrasing. The raw phrasing is often the sharp phrasing.

## Template 5.4 — The Meeting-Rehearsal Script

### `# DOCUMENT`

This is a 3-minute rehearsal script for a specific upcoming meeting.
It's not a monologue — it's what I'd say if the meeting went perfectly.
Three talking points, each 1 minute, in order.

### `# SECTION — The opening 30 seconds`

What I say first. Specific. Names the meeting's purpose without
apologizing for my time or theirs. No "thanks for taking the meeting."

### `# SECTION — The three points (1 min each)`

For each point:
- **Claim:** (one sentence)
- **Evidence:** (one concrete example, numbers if possible)
- **Anticipated objection:** (the pushback I expect)
- **Response:** (one sentence)

### `# SECTION — The close`

The specific ask. One sentence. Not "thoughts?" — a closed question
with two options, or a one-line decision request.

### `# TOKEN`

Prefer: short sentences, present tense, first-person singular.
Avoid: "I wanted to just quickly," "just a heads up," "makes sense?"

## How to pick the right template

| Source material | Use |
|---|---|
| Walk-and-talk voice memo | 5.1 |
| Slack thread with many voices | 5.2 |
| Braindump / stream-of-consciousness | 5.3 |
| You have a specific meeting tomorrow | 5.4 |
