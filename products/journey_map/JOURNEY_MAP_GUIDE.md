# The 31-Touchpoint × 13-Layer Journey Map

## Why journey maps usually fail

Most journey maps are 5 touchpoints (awareness → consideration →
purchase → retention → advocacy) drawn in a slide. They're useless
because the real work lives in the space between the slides —
specifically in the 25+ micro-touchpoints nobody planned.

A 31-touchpoint map forces you to plan the micro-motion. A 13-layer
depth forces you to plan WHAT each touchpoint *does* (captures data?
scores the buyer? routes to the right segment? records feedback?).

Together: 31 × 13 = 403 decisions. Sounds like a lot. Most touchpoints
only use 2–3 layers. But you have to have chosen.

## The 31 touchpoints (arranged in 5 stages)

### Awareness (TP 1–6)
1. Anonymous source (FOTW / feed algorithm surfacing you)
2. First-read content
3. Second-touch content (different channel)
4. Search / intentional re-find
5. Reference check (someone else mentions you)
6. Light signal (follow, subscribe, join)

### Interest (TP 7–13)
7. Direct engagement (comment, DM, reply)
8. Lead magnet / free value download
9. Email opt-in
10. Email #1 (welcome)
11. Email #2 (credibility)
12. Email #3 (anti-avatar)
13. Booking intent (clicks "book a call")

### Desire (TP 14–19)
14. Discovery call
15. Post-call audio overview
16. Proposal / quote
17. Proposal follow-up
18. Objection-handling touch
19. Commitment signal

### Action (TP 20–23)
20. Purchase / contract signed
21. Welcome sequence
22. First deliverable
23. Kickoff check-in

### Retention (TP 24–31)
24. 30-day review
25. Renewal signal (satisfaction)
26. Upsell surface
27. Referral ask
28. Case-study interview
29. Advocacy content (they post about you)
30. Renewal decision
31. Reactivation (if lapsed)

## The 13 layers (what each touchpoint MUST address)

1. **Identity** — who is this person, what avatar
2. **Intent** — what are they actually here for
3. **Data capture** — what do we record
4. **Classification** — what segment do they go into
5. **Scoring** — what's their value/urgency
6. **Routing** — who/what handles them next
7. **Delivery** — what content/action gets sent
8. **Feedback** — did they respond; what signal
9. **Learning** — what do we update based on response
10. **Steward signal** — what does Friday/human do
11. **Measurement** — what KPI this touchpoint moves
12. **Continuity** — next-touchpoint trigger
13. **Reactivation** — what happens if they go silent

## How to fill in the template

Open `TEMPLATE_31_TOUCHPOINTS.md`. For each of your 31 touchpoints:

1. Write one sentence for each applicable layer.
2. If a layer doesn't apply, write "N/A" explicitly. Don't leave blank.
3. If a layer feels forced, the touchpoint may be vestigial. Delete it.

Target: 90 minutes for a first pass. Most founders finish in 2–3 hours
because they discover their funnel has ~8 touchpoints currently, not 31.

## The compound effect

Every touchpoint you map is a touchpoint you can optimize. Every layer
you fill is a decision you don't have to make under pressure at 11pm.
A mapped funnel compounds: every week you find one more layer to tune.

Most businesses never get past TP 14 (discovery call). This document's
job is to remind you that TPs 15–31 are where retention and referral
live.
