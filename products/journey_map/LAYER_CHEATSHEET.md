# The 13 Layers — Cheatsheet

*Print this. Pin it. Use it per touchpoint.*

| # | Layer | Key question | Output format |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identity | Who is this person? | avatar_id |
| 2 | Intent | What are they here for? | one-line intent statement |
| 3 | Data capture | What do we record? | list of fields |
| 4 | Classification | What segment? | segment tag |
| 5 | Scoring | What's their value/urgency? | 1–10 score |
| 6 | Routing | Who handles them next? | agent_id / human |
| 7 | Delivery | What do they receive? | content/action name |
| 8 | Feedback | Did they respond? | yes/no + signal type |
| 9 | Learning | What do we update? | model/weights/memory |
| 10 | Steward signal | What does Friday do? | alert / brief / none |
| 11 | Measurement | What KPI moves? | KPI name |
| 12 | Continuity | Next trigger? | TP + timing |
| 13 | Reactivation | What if silent? | re-engagement plan |

## Reading order per touchpoint

When designing a touchpoint, fill the layers in this order:

1. **Identity + Intent** (who, what) — 30 seconds
2. **Data capture + Classification + Scoring** (how we read them) — 2 min
3. **Routing + Delivery** (what we do) — 2 min
4. **Feedback + Learning** (what we record) — 1 min
5. **Steward signal + Measurement** (what surfaces for humans) — 1 min
6. **Continuity + Reactivation** (what happens next) — 30 seconds

~7 minutes per touchpoint. ~3.5 hours for all 31. Doable in an afternoon.
