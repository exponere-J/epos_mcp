# Brand DNA Profile — Maya Chen, Independent Consultant (composite)

*Completed: illustrative only* · *Maya is a composite for teaching purposes.*

---

## Origin

Maya spent 12 years inside a Big 4 consultancy doing change-management
engagements she increasingly didn't believe in. In 2024 a friend
offered her $8k for a 4-week one-person project. She delivered it in
half the billable hours the firm would have scoped. Her client
referred three more. Maya quit. She exists now because the firm model
charges for hours; she charges for outcomes.

## Audience — Rachel, VP of People at a 200-person SaaS

- **Role:** VP People at a post-Series B SaaS (150-300 employees)
- **Monday 8:30am:** Reviewing exit interviews. 2 senior engineers left
  last week. CEO wants her to have "an answer" by Thursday.
- **What she'd pay $100 to make stop:** Spending 6 hours a week in
  compensation-benchmarking spreadsheets
- **Content she reads:** Lenny's, Reforge, LinkedIn during her commute

## Objections (verbatim)

1. "We already tried a consultant last year. Didn't stick."
2. "My CEO wants a framework, not a 4-week engagement."
3. "Can you work within our existing HRIS or do we need to migrate?"

## Anti-avatar

- This isn't for you if you want someone to redesign your whole org.
  Maya picks one problem at a time.
- This isn't for you if your comp strategy is "pay median." She only
  works with teams willing to stop copying.
- This isn't for you if "4 weeks" sounds like a deadline. It's the
  whole engagement.

## Value pillars (3)

1. **Time-boxed** — 4 weeks, fixed scope, fixed price. Evidence:
   Maya's last 11 engagements all shipped in week 4.
2. **Works the existing system** — no new HRIS, no new vendor.
   Evidence: her process lives in Google Sheets + Slack; clients don't
   need to onboard anything.
3. **Outcome-priced** — you pay for the deliverable, not the hours.
   Evidence: Maya doesn't timesheet. Clients get the output or their
   money back.

## Voice

- **Prefer:** shipped, specific, decision, outcome, constraint
- **Avoid:** strategic, transform, journey, best-practice, leverage,
  thought leader
- **Register:** a senior ex-consultant who's tired of consulting
  theater. Direct, numbers-heavy, slightly impatient with jargon.
