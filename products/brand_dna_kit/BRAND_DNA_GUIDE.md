# The Brand DNA Discovery Method

## From a 90-minute conversation to a 1-page profile you use every day.

---

## 1. Why most brand exercises fail

Most brand discovery produces a 40-page deck. The deck sits on a shelf.
Six weeks later the content still sounds generic because nobody reads
40 pages while writing a LinkedIn post.

Brand DNA inverts that. **One page. Constantly visible. Actually used.**

The method is a 90-minute structured conversation with yourself (or a
client), followed by synthesis through the 4-ring CCP protocol (see
the CCP Pack for the full CCP treatment), output as a single-page
profile.

## 2. The six buckets

Every brand has the same six buckets. The discovery script asks ~4
questions per bucket. You don't answer them in order — answer where
the energy is, skip the rest.

| Bucket | Core question | What you're extracting |
|---|---|---|
| **Origin** | Why does this exist? | The non-obvious reason. Not "help businesses grow." |
| **Audience** | Who feels the problem most acutely? | One specific person, not a segment. |
| **Objections** | What makes them not buy? | Verbatim phrases. |
| **Anti-avatar** | Who should buy but shouldn't? | Explicitly named. |
| **Value pillars** | What do you *do* better? | 3 pillars. With evidence. |
| **Voice** | How do you sound? | Vocabulary preferences + avoidances. |

## 3. The synthesis

After the conversation, fill the `TEMPLATE_BRAND_DNA_PROFILE.md` — in
the speaker's own words as much as possible. Do not paraphrase what
they said into "professional branding language." The raw phrasing is
what makes the profile useful.

Then — and this is the discipline — **delete everything generic**.
"We help businesses succeed" is generic. Delete. "We rebuild the way
a vision-impaired founder runs a two-person company when he can't read
a screen for more than 15 minutes" is specific. Keep.

If you can't name the specific detail, the discovery wasn't deep
enough. Go back to the bucket.

## 4. How the profile gets used

- Content writer reads the profile before drafting any piece
- Sales rep reads it before a discovery call
- New hire reads it during onboarding week 1
- When you're tempted to go generic, you see the profile and stop

That's it. One page. Every day.

## 5. What Brand DNA does NOT do

- It doesn't build your visual brand. That's a different exercise.
- It doesn't write your content. It *constrains* your content.
- It doesn't survive a pivot unchanged. When your audience or offering
  shifts, re-run discovery. Usually takes 30 minutes, not 90, because
  most buckets stay stable.
