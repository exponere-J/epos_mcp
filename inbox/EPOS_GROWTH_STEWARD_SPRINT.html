II. The Stewardship Questionnaire

This is the Soil Test. It replaces a clinical business intake with a shared exploration of the creator's land. Every question feeds a specific system decision.

Part I — The Seed (Vision)

"What is the 'far-fetched' goal? If we successfully brought the fruits of your labor into the storehouse three years from now, what does your daily life look like?"

→ Feeds: 90-Day Plan milestones, monetization targets, content pillar prioritization

"What are the specific cultures, landscapes, stories, or experiences that you feel a calling to share with others?"

→ Feeds: Content pillar definitions, niche positioning, partnership opportunities

"When your community is thriving, what does that look like? How many people? What are they saying to each other? What are they celebrating together?"

→ Feeds: Audience targeting, community platform selection, engagement strategy

Part II — The Climate (Voice & Boundaries)

"Your community is a safe space. Are there specific 'weeds' — topics, energies, types of interactions — that we must ensure never take root in your content or your comments?"

→ Feeds: Guardianship filter rules, brand boundaries, content validation rules

"When you're at your most authentic — the version of yourself that your closest people love — how would you describe that energy? What does it feel like to be around you?"

→ Feeds: Voice profile, caption tone, brand archetype definition

"Is there a message, a lesson, or a feeling that you want every piece of your content to carry — even if it's never stated explicitly?"

→ Feeds: Brand validator rules, content scoring weights, thematic consistency checks

Part III — The Nurturing (Partnership Style)

"How do you best like to be supported? Do you prefer a weekly 'Crop Report' with numbers and insights, or a more conversational touchpoint to see how the 'weather' is feeling?"

→ Feeds: Reporting format, communication cadence, Account Manager tone

"When it comes to content we create on your behalf — clips, captions, posts — how involved do you want to be? Review everything? Trust us after the first month? Something in between?"

→ Feeds: Approval workflow design, autonomy level, escalation thresholds

"What would make you feel like this partnership is working? Not metrics — how would it feel?"

→ Feeds: Success definition beyond numbers, relationship health indicators, renewal signals

III. The 90-Day Growth Plans

Stacey — 90-Day Cultivation Plan

Month 1: Preparing the Soil

Foundation · Consistency · Voice Establishment

Weeks 1-2: The First Planting

Streaming: Lock in 2x/week Twitch live chats (Tue + Thu recommended). Each session 45-60 min. Topics drawn from her natural interests — no forced "content strategy" yet. Just her being her.

Content Production: EPOS produces 8-12 derivative clips per live. Focus on finding her "quotable moments" — the lines and reactions that feel authentically Stacey. First two weeks are calibration: learning what clips she approves vs rejects tells us her real voice.

Platforms: TikTok (3-4 clips/week), IG Reels (3-4 clips/week), IG Stories (daily, low-effort), LinkedIn (1 text post/week from live insights).

Milestone: Consistent posting schedule established. Voice profile calibrated. 100+ followers across platforms.

Weeks 3-4: Roots Taking Hold

Streaming: Add optional 3rd live if energy allows. Begin introducing topic structure — one "pillar per session" (Travel Tuesday, Book Club Thursday). Let the community know what's coming.

Content Production: EPOS increases to 15-20 derivatives per week. Add IG carousels (2x/week: book recs, travel tips). Begin building the caption library specific to her voice.

Community: Start engaging with commenters. Reply to every comment for the first 30 days (or EPOS drafts replies she approves). This builds the core 50 community members who become evangelists.

Milestone: 300+ followers. Engagement rate 8%+. Community members start recognizing each other in comments.

Month 2: The Growth Season

Expansion · Book Club Launch · Affiliate Activation

Weeks 5-6: Book Club Seeding

Launch: Announce the book club during a live. Frame it as a community experience, not a product. "I've been wanting to do this — who's in?" Pick the first book. Create a simple landing page or Discord channel.

Content: Book-related content becomes its own stream within the pillars. Reading recommendation carousels. "What I'm reading this week" Stories. TikTok clips of her reacting to passages.

Monetization: Activate Amazon Associates / Bookshop.org affiliate links on every book recommendation. This is the first revenue — organic, non-pushy, and directly tied to her authentic interests.

Milestone: 20+ book club members. First affiliate revenue ($50-200). Weekly book club content producing 5+ additional clips.

Weeks 7-8: Travel Pillar Expansion

Content: Dedicated travel content — planning tips, destination spotlights, "dream trip" discussions during lives. If she has travel photos/videos, repurpose into TikTok and IG content.

Monetization: Research travel affiliate programs. Approach one travel brand for initial conversation (not pitching — just introduction, "I'm building a community around travel and culture").

Community: Book club active. Start "Stories for Zayne" as a recurring weekly moment in lives. This becomes the emotional anchor content that drives deepest engagement.

Milestone: 700+ followers. Affiliate revenue $200-500/month. Travel brand conversation initiated. Community members contributing topic suggestions.

Month 3: Early Harvest

Monetization Activation · Partnership Readiness · Community Deepening

Weeks 9-10: Revenue Streams Online

Monetization: Multiple affiliate streams active (books, travel gear, lifestyle products she genuinely uses). First brand inquiry or outreach conversation. Twitch subs beginning to trickle in.

Content: 20-25 pieces/week running smoothly. EPOS handles calendar, captions, scheduling. Stacey reviews and approves in under 15 min/day.

Authority: LinkedIn content establishing her as a thoughtful voice on parenting and personal growth. Not "influencer" — "person worth listening to."

Weeks 11-12: Storehouse Assessment

Review: Full 90-day Crop Report. What grew. What didn't take. What the soil tells us about the next season.

Plan: Draft the next 90-day plan based on real data. Community feedback informs next quarter's content pillars.

Milestone: 1,000+ followers. $500-1,000/month revenue. Established community with recurring members. Clear data on which pillars perform best.

Desiree — 90-Day Amplification Plan

Consistency · Clip Discovery · Algorithm Training

RAG Architecture (How the Dashboard Gets Smart)

The dashboard isn't just a display — it's a conversational interface powered by Retrieval-Augmented Generation. The creator can ask questions and get answers grounded in their actual data.

How RAG Works Here

Data Layer (What Gets Indexed):

→ All platform analytics (follower counts, engagement rates, post performance)

→ Content calendar history (what was posted when, what performed, what was rejected)

→ Questionnaire answers (the creator's voice, goals, boundaries)

→ Mirror Report (brand analysis and opportunities)

→ 90-Day Plan (milestones, timeline, targets)

→ Weekly Crop Reports (historical performance summaries)

→ Market sentiment data (trending topics, algorithm changes, niche activity)

Query Layer (What the Creator Can Ask):

→ "What's working best for me right now?" → RAG retrieves top performers + echolocation scores + engagement patterns

→ "Am I on track for my month 2 goals?" → RAG retrieves 90-Day Plan milestones + current metrics + trajectory

→ "What should I talk about on my next live?" → RAG retrieves trending topics in niche + community questions + underserved pillars

→ "How is my book club growing?" → RAG retrieves book club metrics + engagement + affiliate revenue from book recs

Response Layer (How It Answers):

→ Every answer grounded in the creator's actual data, not generic advice

→ Responses use the agricultural language framework ("Your travel content is the fastest-growing crop this season")

→ Always action-oriented: never just data, always "here's what to do with this information"

V. Personalized Outreach: The Stewardship Approach

You don't sell. You see. You reflect. You offer to cultivate.

The Outreach Sequence

Step 1: Research (Silent — They Don't Know You're Looking)

Run the Digital Presence Research Template on a prospect. Understand their brand, their audience, their content patterns, and their untapped opportunities. This takes 30-60 minutes per prospect.

Step 2: The First Touch (A Gift, Not A Pitch)

Send them something useful — an observation about their content that shows you actually watched and understood it. Not flattery. Insight.

"I've been watching your streams and I noticed something — your [specific moment type] clips get 3-4x the engagement of your other content, but you're only posting 2 of them per week. The algorithm is literally telling you what it wants from you. I think there's a path here that could change your trajectory."

No mention of EPOS. No pitch. Just a person who sees what they're building and has an insight to share.

Step 3: The Offer (If They Respond)

If they engage, offer the Mirror Report as a free gift.

"I'd love to put together something more complete for you — a brand portrait that shows you how your audience sees you, where the biggest opportunities are, and what a 90-day growth path could look like. No strings. I just think you're building something worth investing in."

Step 4: The Mirror (The Deliverable)

Deliver the Mirror Report. Let them sit with it. Don't follow up for 3-5 days. If the report is good — if it truly shows them themselves with clarity — they will come back to you.

Step 5: The Partnership (Only When They're Ready)

When they ask "how do we do this?" — THAT is when you describe the stewardship model. Not before. The pitch only lands after they've experienced the value.

"We find the plot, test the soil, plant the seeds, and nurture the crop until harvest. Your only job is to show up and be yourself. We handle everything else. Want to see what a 90-day plan looks like for you specifically?"

VI. The Weekly Crop Report

The recurring touchpoint that keeps the partnership alive. Data-backed but human-centered.

Crop Report Template

🌱 What Grew This Week

[Top 3 wins — specific posts, follower milestones, engagement records, community moments. Celebrate the tangible.]

☀️ What Needs More Sun

[1-2 areas that underperformed or need attention. Framed as opportunity, not failure. "Your book content is rich but posting at 6pm — shifting to 11am could catch the lunchtime scroll."]

🌾 What We're Planting Next

[Next week's content calendar preview. Key posts. Any new experiments. What the data suggests trying.]

🌡️ The Climate

[Market sentiment summary. Any trending topics relevant to their pillars. Algorithm changes to be aware of. Framed as "weather report," not overwhelming data.]

📊 The Numbers

[Simple metrics: followers (with +/- from last week), engagement rate, top post, content published vs planned, revenue if applicable. Always compared to THEIR own last week, never to anyone else.]
