Case Study — PGP (Pressure-Washing Operation)

Period: 2025-09 to 2026-02. 5 months, solo operator.

Before

I ran the business manually. Invoicing took 8 minutes per job × 40 jobs/week = 5.3 hours/week just in paperwork. I missed two follow-ups in October because I forgot who I'd promised what. By November my eyes hurt by 11 AM and I was forcing myself through another 4 hours of screen work every evening.

The intervention

I built EPOS as a side organism while running PGP. Specifically:

  1. Wave (invoicing) — automated from completed-job signal
  2. BrowserUse (Chrome extension era) — scraping review sites weekly, surfacing anything negative
  3. Friday (chief of staff) — 05:30 morning briefing: 4 layers (health, client, market, personal)
  4. Supervisory Monitor — client state machine (LEAD → DISCOVERY → ACTIVE → RETAINED)

After

Invoicing dropped from 5.3 hours/week to 12 minutes/week. Follow-up rate: 100%. Screen time: 90 minutes/day total across PGP + EPOS work — down from 6+ hours. Revenue increased 22% in 90 days because I was actually following up on quotes I used to forget.

The generalizable lesson

A solo operator doesn't need a bigger team. They need an organism that remembers what they don't have time to track, and that responds to signals they don't have time to notice. EPOS is that organism. PGP is proof.

What I'd tell a solo operator considering this

You won't know which parts will compound hardest until 60 days in. Start with the pain you feel most acutely. For me it was invoicing. For you it might be follow-ups, or review monitoring, or scheduling. Pick one. Automate to completion. Measure. Then pick the next one.
