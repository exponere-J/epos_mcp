I built the thing I needed.

In 2025 I started losing my vision. I couldn't read a screen for more than 15 minutes without a migraine. I was running a pressure- washing business I couldn't see the invoices for, and trying to learn software engineering when most software assumes you can stare at a terminal all day.

I didn't want accommodations. I wanted an organism that ran the business with me and read the screens when I couldn't.

That's EPOS. It's not software. It's a nervous system for solo-operator businesses — built by someone who couldn't use everyone else's software the normal way.

The discipline that falls out of that constraint — audio-first, voice-dictation, zero-screen-time as a design goal — turns out to help everyone who uses the system. Not just vision-impaired founders. Anyone whose best thinking happens on foot.

What I believe

  • Proprioception precedes intelligence. Your system has to know itself before it can reason about the world.
  • Discipline compounds faster than novelty. A governance gate that prevents 10% of deploys from breaking compounds into 37x improvement a year.
  • The constraint is the gift. Every limitation I hit shaped EPOS into something more general. Vision loss forced voice-first. Time poverty forced compound discipline.

What I don't believe

  • That AI will "transform your business." It won't, on its own. What transforms is discipline, enforced by tools.
  • That faster is better. Reversible is better. Governance- gated is better.
  • That more is better. The right number of prompts is fewer, better-structured. The right number of dashboards is one, always- visible.
